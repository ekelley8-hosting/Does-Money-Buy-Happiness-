    Motivation of this project

    Our project is motivated by the need to quantify the true cost of happiness. We aim to identify the thresholds and trade-offs. By integrating cost of living, quality of life and factors such as unemployment and crime rate data, we aim to correlate the nonlinear connections that impacts well-being. The target audience for our project data to be used id for post graduate student to guide them which county in this country would satisfy their happiness the most.

    This page will provide further evauluation.

    Data Source 1

    Description:
    Data source 1 includes the foundational information regarding costs based on varying family sizes, and what certain “costs” of living go toward (food, housing, etc.).

    Data Source 2

    Description:
    Data Source 2 goes beyond generic data and provides the crime rate and unemployment. This allowed us to conduct a multivariate analysis and correlate public/economic security with subjective well-being. .

    Data Source 3

    Description:
    Data Source 3 shows insight into multiple factors of quality of life, such as stability, rights, and climate, which allowed us to evaluate the effect of nonfinancial factors on the trend of quality of life. By this we were able to determine if high costs in the US are correlated with stability and if other countries provide a better quality of life than the US for a lower financial price.
